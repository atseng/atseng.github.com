    • Salvaged Future Installation

    Salvaged Future is an installation designed to examine the social context behind striving for a meangingful existence. Based on accounts by activist Dylan Lazerow - it is a historical, sociological, and economic collection of obseravations addressing the driving forces which perpetuate cycles of urban violence.

    Assembled in five days with artist Allen Tseng, Salvaged Future is the manifestation of a vessel for navigating psychological and sociological themes - the creation of a ship to transverse older paradigms of thought. At its core, it is about the embrace of loss. The materials salvaged for this debut installation, like the urban communities stricken by poverty and violence around them, existed long before they were collected.

    Their stories have imprinted them with their character - rusted, worn, peeling and raw. Salvaged Future aims to see these materials with their cracks, crevices and scars - as narratives each with their own history stretching deep like roots. And we revel in these flaws, because it reminds us all of the stories that humans beings all live - the historic cycles of creation, destruction, of picking up the pieces and making something new in that fracture between the world we see and the world we hope for.

    Salvaged Future attempts to share through the experience of collaboration a transformative energy. Not to use tragedy as an excuse to succumb to anger, rather art as an alternative to violence.
